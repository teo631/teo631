The part that I understood the best compared to other parts: SPRINGS & ROLLBARS

You know the things that go boing? They follow a simple rule (shout-outs to Robert Hooke for finding this formula). "x" is the travel distance of the spring and the "k" is the constant/stiffnes also don't forget delta time since this is a continious force.

f=-xk*△t

Tip: Don't forget to clamp the spring force so it doesn't go into the negatives.Spring cant pull the car down!(Unless you have your wheels glued to ground or something).

But when we apply this to our car you will see that the car is bouncing in place. To fix that we need to dampen the springs. We just need to subtract the travel distance since last step multipled by our damping stiffnes. x for the spring size and k for the damper stiffnes. Note that we are not using delta time here since this is a action-reaction type of function.

f_damper=-△x*k

Well lets get into rollbars. You might not understand their purpuse rn and see them as "unnecesery". Youc can always see the lateral friction part and come back later.

Lets start. x1 is the lenght of the right spring on the axel. x2 is for the right spring.

rollbarForce=(x1-x2)*k*△t
